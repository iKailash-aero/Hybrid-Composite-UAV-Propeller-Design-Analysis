# Hybrid Composite UAV Propeller Design and Analysis

## Project Overview

This project focuses on the design, aerodynamic evaluation, and structural analysis of a 10×8 inch UAV propeller developed using hybrid composite materials. The objective was to improve structural integrity, reduce deformation, and enhance strength-to-weight ratio while maintaining aerodynamic performance.

## Objectives

* Design and optimize a UAV propeller using NACA 2412 airfoil geometry
* Compare conventional, modified, and redesigned propeller configurations
* Perform CFD analysis to evaluate aerodynamic performance
* Conduct Fluid Structure Interaction (FSI) analysis to determine structural response
* Investigate the influence of CFRP, GFRP, Kevlar, and hybrid composites on propeller performance
* Identify the optimum geometry-material combination for UAV applications

## Software Used

* ANSYS Fluent
* ANSYS Static Structural
* ANSYS Material Designer
* CAD Modelling Tools

## Methodology

1. Development of conventional and redesigned propeller geometries
2. CFD simulation using ANSYS Fluent
3. Extraction of aerodynamic loads
4. Transfer of pressure loads through FSI workflow
5. Structural analysis for deformation, stress, and strain
6. Material comparison study
7. Hybrid composite development and evaluation

## CFD Boundary Conditions

* Inlet Velocity: 4.71 m/s
* Atmospheric Pressure: 101325 Pa
* Turbulence Model: k-epsilon
* Pressure-Based Solver

## Structural Analysis

The aerodynamic pressure distribution obtained from CFD was imported into the structural environment. Total deformation, equivalent stress, and equivalent strain were evaluated for different material configurations.

## Materials Evaluated

### Base Materials

* Carbon/Epoxy UD
* Carbon/Epoxy Woven
* E-Glass/Epoxy UD
* Epoxy Carbon UD
* Epoxy Carbon Woven
* Epoxy E-Glass
* S-Glass
* FR4
* Kevlar 49 Epoxy UD

### Hybrid Composites

* CFRP-GFRP
* CFRP-KFRP
* KFRP-GFRP

## Key Outcomes

* Redesigned propeller achieved improved structural performance compared to conventional configuration.
* Hybrid composites demonstrated enhanced strength-to-weight characteristics.
* Stress concentration near blade-root regions was significantly reduced.
* Composite material selection strongly influenced deformation and stiffness behaviour.
